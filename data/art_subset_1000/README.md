## Metadata Format
The `metadata.csv` file contains:

| Column | Description | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| `image_id` | Unique sequential ID | real_0001, fake_0234 |
| `image_filename` | Original filename | 0_000045.png, 1_125016.png |
| `file_path` | Relative path in subset | images/real/classical_art/0_000045.png |
| `true_label` | Ground truth label | real, fake |
| `generator_model` | Generator or "human" | human, dalle, stable_diffusion, etc. |
| `art_style` | Art style category | realism, surrealism, impressionism, etc. |
| `source_folder` | Original source path | /data/raw/art_dataset/real/classical_art |

## Research Applications

### Study 1: Human Detection Performance by Art Style
Analyze whether humans perform worse on certain art styles:
- Compare human accuracy on realism vs. surrealism
- Test if impressionism is harder to detect than baroque
- Identify which styles are most confusing to humans

### Study 2: AI Detector Performance by Generator & Style
Analyze whether AI detectors fail on specific generator-style combinations:
- Does detector performance degrade differently for Stable Diffusion vs. DALL-E?
- Is the detector more fooled by classical_style AI images vs. modern_style?
- Which generator + style combination is hardest to detect?

### Study 3: Cross-Modal Analysis
- Is human performance more affected by style or generator?
- Is AI detector performance more affected by generator or style?
- Do they fail on the same images?

## Sampling Methodology
- **Real Images**: Stratified random sampling (25 per classical style, balanced across categories)
- **AI Images**: Stratified by generator (83-84 each), with style preservation from original data
- **Random Seed**: 42 (reproducible)
- **Purpose**: Enable fair comparison of performance across styles and generators
